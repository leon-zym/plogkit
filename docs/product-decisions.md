# Product Decisions

## App Name

The product name is **PlogKit**.

## Current Slogan

Current working slogan:

> A lightweight plog toolkit beyond your system Photos app.

The slogan is acceptable for now, but may still be refined before launch.

## Positioning

PlogKit is a lightweight plog capability extension for the system Photos app.

The core idea:

> The system Photos app handles photo adjustment and color tuning. PlogKit handles text, backgrounds, stitching, and final export preparation before posting to social platforms.

This positioning intentionally avoids the market category of heavy photo beautification tools. PlogKit is not meant to compete with products centered around filters, beauty editing, body reshaping, effects, or large template and asset systems.

## Target Users

Primary users:

- Plog creators.
- Users who often post image-based stories or photo diaries.
- Users who want lightweight formatting after editing photos in the system Photos app.
- Users who dislike heavy photo editors with many unused features, subscriptions, ads, or large storage footprint.

The first version should serve users who already have selected photos and need a fast way to make them publishable.

## Product Principles

- Stay lightweight through feature restraint.
- Complement the system Photos app instead of replacing it.
- Keep editing local-first.
- Prioritize fast completion of common plog tasks.
- Avoid requiring login for basic usage.
- Avoid watermarks on normal export.
- Prefer a small set of polished controls over many weak features.
- Do not turn the app into a general-purpose design or beautification suite.

## Core User Jobs

PlogKit should help users:

- Add readable, good-looking text to photos.
- Add margins, borders, rounded corners, and background colors.
- Stitch multiple photos into vertical or grid compositions.
- Prepare images for social publishing with export presets.
- Recover from mistakes through undo and redo.
- Continue editing after accidental app exit or export.

## Product Boundary

PlogKit is closer to a **photo finishing toolkit** than a traditional photo editor.

It should not be described only as a layout tool, because the scope includes text, background, borders, stitching, and export optimization. It should also not be described as a full image editor, because that implies filters, retouching, and beautification features that are intentionally out of scope.

Useful positioning phrases:

- Lightweight plog toolkit.
- System Photos companion.
- Photo finishing toolkit.
- Plog publishing helper.
- Lightweight image formatting tool.
